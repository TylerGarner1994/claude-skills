## Headline Formulas

### "How To" Headlines

Most reliable format. Promise specific transformation.

**Template:** "How To [Achieve Desired Outcome] Without [Dreaded Obstacle]"

**Examples:**
- "How To Fall Asleep In 15 Minutes Without Pills, Meditation, or Lying Awake For Hours"
- "How To Support Healthy Blood Pressure Naturally Without Giving Up Salt or Your Favorite Foods"
- "How To Look 10 Years Younger Without Surgery, Injections, or Expensive Creams"

### "Secret" Headlines

Create exclusivity and insider knowledge appeal.

**Template:** "The Secret [Benefit/Method] That [Authority Figures] [Action]"

**Examples:**
- "The Secret Energy Formula That Olympic Athletes Swear By"
- "The Secret Joint-Support Compound Orthopedic Surgeons Take Themselves"
- "The Secret of 80-Year-Olds With the Memory of 40-Year-Olds"

### Question Headlines

Demand engagement. Best for problem-aware audiences.

**Template:** "Do You [Make This Common Mistake / Have This Symptom / Want This Outcome]?"

**Examples:**
- "Do You Make These 7 Common Supplement Mistakes?"
- "Is Your 'Healthy' Breakfast Actually Accelerating Aging?"
- "Why Do Some People Over 60 Have More Energy Than 30-Year-Olds?"

### News Headlines

Leverage authority and timeliness.

**Template:** "[News Source/Authority] [Discovery Verb] [Benefit/Breakthrough]"

**Examples:**
- "Breaking: Harvard Study Links This $3 Nutrient to Memory Protection"
- "New Stanford Research Reveals Why Most Probiotics Don't WorkГўВЂВ”And What Does"

### Command Headlines

Direct and actionable. Best for most-aware audiences.

**Template:** "[Action Verb] [Negative] ГўВЂВ” [Action Verb] [Positive]"

**Examples:**

### Reason-Why Headlines

Leverage curiosity about causation.

**Template:** "Why [Surprising Fact About Common Belief/Practice]"

**Examples:**
- "Why 95% of Calcium Supplements Actually WEAKEN Your Bones"
- "Why Your Multivitamin Is Probably Making You MORE Deficient"

**Template:** "[Specific Result Statement]ГўВЂВ”[Name, Age/Credentials]"

**Template:** "If You [Have This Condition/Situation], Here's [Benefit/Solution]"

**Examples:**
- "If You're Over 50 With Low Energy, Here's WhyГўВЂВ”And How To Fix It"

## Bullet Writing Rules

1. **One idea per bullet** ГўВЂВ” Don't dilute with multiple claims
2. **Specific beats vague** ГўВЂВ” "47%" beats "significant improvement"
3. **Curiosity over information** ГўВЂВ” Tease, don't reveal
4. **Emotional hook** ГўВЂВ” Connect to feelings, not just facts
5. **Page numbers add credibility** ГўВЂВ” Implies detailed proof inside
6. **Vary patterns** ГўВЂВ” Mix blind, secret, why, imagine, warning, question
7. **Front-load strongest bullets** ГўВЂВ” Lead with most compelling
8. **15-25 bullets typical** ГўВЂВ” More for long-form, fewer for short

### Testing Bullets

Before finalizing, ask:
- Does this create genuine curiosity?
- Would I click/read more to learn this?
- Is it specific enough to be believable?
- Does it connect to a real desire or fear?
- Is it different from the bullet above and below it?
